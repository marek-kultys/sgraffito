Technique

Sgraffito mural is made by applying several layers of tinted mortar one on top of another, onto a wettened masonry or concrete wall. Then selected parts of front layers are removed by scratching to reveal coloured layers underneath into a designed image. Because a sgraffito mural is made of mortar, it provides both protection for the wall and unique decoration for the space.

Sgraffito murals are known for their durability and versatility. Depending on the context, space, environment, and your own preferences sgraffito murals can be adapted for virtually any situation.

  • Suitable for indoor and outdoor spaces

  • Water and moisture resistant (OK for bathrooms)

  • Any size and any shape possible

  • Suitable for most walls (masonry, concrete, etc.)

  • Wide range of colours and finishes available

  • Can be used with fittings and installations

  • Almost any design possible

Materials and Processes

I work with traditional techniques of old masters that are proven to last for decades (and even longer).

I use natural mortar materials that are safe, durable, and non-toxic.

I use only best pigments available (natural or specialty pigments on request, e.g. the famous ochres from Roussillon, Provence.)

I do everything by hand and you can witness and participate in the process of creating your own sgraffito.

Well Known Sgraffito Murals by Other Artists

Sgraffito is known to have been used since the antiquity. It was revived and popularised during the Renaissance age. Today, few artists master the technique, so every new sgraffito mural is not only unique for its design, but also rare for the technique. Here are some excellent examples of sgraffito art that I admire by mural painters from around the world.
