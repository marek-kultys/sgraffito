The word “sgraffito” comes from the Italian “graffiare” which means “to scratch”.

The technique of sgraffito

Sgraffito mural is made by applying several layers of tinted plaster one on top of another, onto a wettened masonry or concrete wall. Then selected parts of front layers are removed by scratching to reveal coloured layers underneath into a designed image. Because a sgraffito mural is made of plaster, it provides both protection for the wall and unique decoration for the space.

Sgraffito murals are known for their durability and versatility. Depending on the context, space, environment, and your own preferences sgraffito murals can be adapted for virtually any situation.

  • Indoor & outdoor sgraffito

  • Any size & any shape

  • Most masonry & concrete walls

  • Multiple colours & finishes

  • OK for fittings & installations

  • Pictorial & geometrical designs
